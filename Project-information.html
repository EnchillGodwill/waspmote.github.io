Project description

Recent reports indicate that activities such as farming along river bodies, cattle rearing and artisanal mining in river bodies contribute to the high levels of pollution in Ghana. This project, therefore, seeks to monitor physical and chemical river quality parameters using wireless sensor devices. The parameters measured include pH, calcium ion (Ca2+), electrical conductivity (EC), dissolved oxygen (DO), silver (Ag+), fluoride ion (F-), nitrate ion (NO3-), oxidation-reduction potential (ORP), and temperature. Monitoring these quality parameters would provide stakeholders and water regulatory bodies with real-time information and early warning signals of state of the river. The use of wireless sensor devices for river water monitoring ensures high data precision, data accuracy, timely reporting. In this project, five (5) sensor boards with different probes are deployed at the Weija water intake in the Central Region of Ghana. The types of probes used for the real-time river monitoring are listed in table below. The data obtained is transmitted through a 4G Communication Gateway to a central repository on a web portal and displayed for stakeholders to view and provide early warning signals.
